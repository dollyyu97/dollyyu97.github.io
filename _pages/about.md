I am a PhD candidate in Financial Economics at the [Yale School of Management](https://som.yale.edu), advised by [James Choi](https://som.yale.edu/faculty-research/faculty-directory/james-j-choi), [Andrew Metrick](https://som.yale.edu/faculty-research/faculty-directory/andrew-metrick), [Boris Vallée](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/profile.aspx?facId=1015798), and [Paul Goldsmith-Pinkham](https://paulgp.github.io/).

My research lies at the intersection of **household finance** and **financial innovation**. I study how households manage liquidity constraints and navigate novel financial products, and how financial institutions respond to funding shocks.

Prior to Yale, I was a Research Associate at Harvard Business School, where I worked with Professors Boris Vallée and Jonas Heese on research in finance and accounting.
